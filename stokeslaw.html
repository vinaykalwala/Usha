Settling Velocity Calculator (Stokes' Law)

Note:

This calculator determines the Settling Velocity using Stokes' Law, which is crucial for analyzing how particles settle in a fluid.

It is widely used in water treatment, environmental studies, and industrial separation processes to optimize sedimentation and filtration systems.

What is Stokes Law

Stokes' Law describes the force of viscosity acting on a small spherical object moving through a fluid.

Why Environment-Sector

It helps analyze sedimentation and particle settling in fluids, which is crucial for water treatment, wastewater management, river sedimentation studies, and pollution control

Explanation of Parameters:

  • Particle Density (ρₚ): The density of the settling particle, measured in kg/m³.
  • Fluid Density (ρf): The density of the fluid in which the particle is settling, measured in kg/m³.
  • Particle Diameter (d): The size of the particle, measured in meters.
  • Fluid Viscosity (μ): The resistance of the fluid to flow, measured in Pa·s.
  • Settling Velocity (vₜ): The speed at which the particle settles in the fluid, measured in m/s.

Why Settling Velocity is Important?

Settling velocity helps in **designing sedimentation tanks**, **predicting sediment transport**, and **optimizing industrial separation processes**.

Validations:

  • Applicability: This formula applies to small, spherical particles in a low-turbulence fluid.
  • Particle Density > Fluid Density: The particle must be denser than the fluid to settle.
  • Positive Values Only: All input parameters must be positive for a valid calculation.
  • Limitations: This formula assumes laminar flow and does not apply to turbulent or irregularly shaped particles.

Real-life Applications:

  • Water Treatment: Designing sedimentation basins for effective filtration.
  • Environmental Studies: Analyzing sediment transport in rivers and lakes.
  • Industrial Processes: Separating particles in mining, oil refining, and food industries.
  • Pharmaceutical Industry: Understanding particle behavior in liquid suspensions.
  • Geology & Soil Science: Studying how soil particles settle in different environments.

Conclusion:

The Settling Velocity calculation is a **key tool in fluid-particle interaction analysis**. Understanding it allows engineers and scientists to **improve separation efficiency, optimize designs, and enhance environmental assessments**.
