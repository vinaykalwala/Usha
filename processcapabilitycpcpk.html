Process Capability (Cp, Cpk) Calculator

Understanding the Parameters

The Process Capability Calculator helps determine how well a process can produce outputs within specified limits. It calculates the Cp (potential capability) and Cpk (actual capability) indices, which are critical for quality control and process improvement. Maintaining high Cp and Cpk values indicates a consistent and reliable process, ensuring better product quality and reduced defects.

Understanding Cp (Process Capability)

The Cp index measures the potential capability of a process by comparing the spread of the process output to the specification limits. A higher Cp value indicates that the process variability is within acceptable limits, leading to consistent product quality.

Understanding Cpk (Process Capability Index)

The Cpk index considers both the process variability and how centered the process is between specification limits. A higher Cpk value reflects better alignment with quality standards, reducing the likelihood of producing defective products.

  • Upper Specification Limit (USL): The maximum acceptable value for the process output.
  • Lower Specification Limit (LSL): The minimum acceptable value for the process output.
  • Process Mean (μ): The average value of the process output.
  • Standard Deviation (σ): A measure of the variability in the process output.
