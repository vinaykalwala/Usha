Gout Diagnosis Calculator - Explanation and Use

Gout is a type of arthritis caused by a build-up of uric acid crystals in the joints, leading to sudden and severe pain, swelling, and redness. It most commonly affects the big toe. Diagnosis is based on symptoms, blood tests for high uric acid levels, and confirming the presence of urate crystals in joint fluid. Treatment focuses on pain relief during attacks and preventing future flare-ups by managing uric acid levels.

Gout Diagnosis Criteria

Gout (or podagra) is characterized by joint inflammation, typically affecting the first metatarsophalangeal joint. The following factors contribute to the likelihood of a gout diagnosis:

  • Painful joint inflammation: The most common symptom is red, swollen, and tender joints, especially the big toe joint.
  • Elevated uric acid levels: High serum uric acid is associated with gout. A level above 5.88 mg/dL increases the likelihood of gout.
  • Male sex: Gout is more common in men, which is considered a risk factor.
  • Previous arthritis attack: A history of arthritis increases the risk of gout.
  • Hypertension or cardiovascular disease (CVD): A history of hypertension or CVD increases the likelihood of gout.
  • Sudden onset: Gout attacks often have a rapid onset, with symptoms appearing within one day.
