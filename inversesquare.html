What is Inverse Square Law

The inverse square law is a principle in physics stating that a physical quantity (such as light, sound, gravity, or electromagnetic force) decreases in intensity proportionally to the square of the distance from the source.

Explanation of Parameters:

  • I₂ (Radiation Intensity at Location 2): The known intensity at a given distance from the source.
  • R₁ (Distance at Location 1): The distance from the source where intensity needs to be calculated.
  • R₂ (Distance at Location 2): The reference distance from the source where I₂ is known.
  • I₁ (Radiation Intensity at Location 1): The calculated intensity at R₁ using the inverse square law.
