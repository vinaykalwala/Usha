Fold of Increase Calculator

Note:

This formula calculates the fold of increase, measuring how much the well's productivity improves after hydraulic fracturing. A higher fold of increase indicates a more effective fracturing process. The equation compares the productivity of a fractured well against a non-fractured well.

Explanation of Parameters:

  • re (Drainage Radius): The area from which the well extracts fluids. A larger value means a wider reservoir.
  • rw (Wellbore Radius): The radius of the drilled well, affecting fluid inflow to the wellbore.
  • Sf (Skin Factor): Represents flow resistance near the well. A high Sf indicates formation damage.

Why Do Some Inputs Give Unexpected Results?

  • If re = rw, then ln(re / rw) = ln(1) = 0, making the formula divide by zero.
  • This leads to a result of zero, which is not meaningful in this context.
  • For valid results, re must be greater than rw.

Valid Input Guidelines:

  • re > rw: Drainage radius should always be larger than wellbore radius for a meaningful calculation.
  • rw > 0: Wellbore radius must be greater than zero, as a well cannot have zero size.
  • Sf: Can be any real number, but extreme values may distort results.

Real-Life Applications:

  • Petroleum Industry: Determines how effective hydraulic fracturing is in increasing oil and gas production.
  • Environmental Engineering: Evaluates underground water extraction efficiency.
  • Geothermal Energy: Assesses the impact of well stimulation on geothermal reservoirs.
  • Mining Operations: Helps optimize fluid extraction from rock formations.
