A Body Shape Index (ABSI) Calculator

What is A Body Shape Index (ABSI)?

ABSI is a measure of body shape that uses waist circumference, height, and weight to assess health risks associated with body shape. Unlike BMI, ABSI accounts for fat distribution, making it a better predictor of mortality risk.

What is ABSI z-score?

The ABSI z-score compares your ABSI to a reference population of the same age and sex. It indicates how your body shape compares to the average:

  • Low z-score: Lower risk of health issues.
  • Average z-score: Moderate risk.
  • High z-score: Higher risk of premature mortality.

Why is ABSI Important?

ABSI is a powerful tool for assessing health risks because it considers fat distribution, which is a key factor in conditions like:

  • Cardiovascular disease
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Metabolic syndrome
  • Premature mortality

Interpreting Your Results

Based on your ABSI z-score:

  • z-score < -1: Low risk of premature mortality.
  • -1 ≤ z-score ≤ 1: Average risk.
  • 1 < z-score ≤ 2: Moderate risk.
  • 2 < z-score ≤ 3: High risk.
  • z-score > 3: Very high risk.

What Can You Do?

If your ABSI z-score indicates a high risk:

  • Consult a Doctor: Seek professional medical advice for a comprehensive health evaluation.
  • Adopt a Healthy Lifestyle: Focus on a balanced diet, regular exercise, and stress management.
  • Monitor Progress: Track your waist circumference, weight, and overall health regularly.
