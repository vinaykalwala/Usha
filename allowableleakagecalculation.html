Allowable Leakage and Additional Leakage Calculation

Note:

Allowable Leakage Calculation helps determine the maximum allowable leakage in a pipeline during pressure testing. The calculation is based on the length of the pipe, its nominal diameter, and the average test pressure applied during the test. The formula takes into account these parameters to provide the allowable leakage in gallons per hour.

Additional Leakage Calculation is used when testing against closed metal-seated valves. The additional leakage allowed is proportional to the nominal size of the valve. This calculation is added to the allowable leakage if a valve size is provided.

Understanding the Parameters:

  • Length of Pipe Tested (Feet): The total length of the pipeline being tested.
  • Nominal Diameter of Pipe (Inches): The diameter of the pipe being tested.
  • Average Test Pressure (PSI): The average pressure applied during the leakage test.
  • Nominal Valve Size (Inches): The size of the closed metal-seated valve (if applicable).

Enter the required values and click "Calculate Leakage" to determine the allowable leakage and additional leakage (if applicable).
