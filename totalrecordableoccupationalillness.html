Total Recordable Occupational Illness Frequency (TRCF-ILL) Calculator

Note:

Total Recordable Occupational Illness Frequency (TRCF-ILL) measures the rate of workplace illnesses per 1 million work hours. It is an important metric for understanding long-term health risks in a work environment.

Understanding the Parameters:

  • Total Recordable Occupational Illness Cases: The number of work-related illnesses that required medical treatment beyond first aid, restricted work, or lost time.
  • Total Work Hours: The total number of hours worked by all employees, including overtime and shifts.

Examples of Occupational Illnesses:

  • Respiratory diseases (e.g., silicosis, asbestosis, occupational asthma)
  • Hearing loss due to prolonged noise exposure
  • Skin conditions (e.g., occupational dermatitis, chemical burns)
  • Musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs) due to repetitive work
  • Toxic exposure-related illnesses (e.g., lead poisoning, chemical poisoning)
