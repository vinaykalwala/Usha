About Diabetes Risk

This diabetes risk calculator estimates your likelihood of developing type 2 diabetes over the next 7.5 years based on clinical and demographic data.

Understanding the Parameters

  • Age: Increasing age is a significant risk factor for developing diabetes. Older adults are generally at higher risk.
  • Gender: Biological differences between males and females can influence diabetes risk, with certain risks varying by sex.
  • Height and Weight (BMI): Body Mass Index (BMI) is calculated from height and weight. Higher BMI is linked to increased diabetes risk.
  • Family History of Diabetes: Having close relatives with diabetes increases your likelihood of developing the condition.
  • Ethnicity: Some ethnic groups, such as Latin Americans, are at higher risk due to genetic and lifestyle factors.
  • Fasting Glucose: Elevated fasting glucose levels indicate impaired glucose regulation, an early sign of diabetes risk.
  • Systolic Blood Pressure: High blood pressure often coexists with diabetes and increases cardiovascular risk.
  • HDL Cholesterol: Low levels of "good" HDL cholesterol are associated with a higher risk of diabetes.

Preventing Diabetes

  • Maintain a healthy, balanced diet.
  • Exercise regularly and stay active.
  • Keep a healthy body weight.
  • Control your blood pressure.
  • Avoid smoking and manage other health conditions.
