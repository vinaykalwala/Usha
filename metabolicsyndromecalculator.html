Understanding Metabolic Syndrome

Metabolic syndrome, also known as insulin resistance syndrome or syndrome X, is a set of common health problems that greatly raise the risk of developing type 2 diabetes and heart disease. These conditions often occur together, making them more dangerous.

In simpler terms, metabolic syndrome refers to a combination of factors such as high blood sugar, high blood pressure, unhealthy cholesterol levels, and extra fat around the waist, all of which increase the chances of serious health issues like diabetes and heart issues.

Metabolic syndrome is diagnosed when you have at least 3 of the 5 risk factors listed above. Having metabolic syndrome increases your risk for:

  • Type 2 diabetes (5x higher risk)
  • Heart disease (2x higher risk)
  • Stroke
  • Fatty liver disease

How to Use the Metabolic Syndrome Calculator

Our metabolic syndrome calculator is simple to use. Just check which of the five criteria apply to you:

  • Waist circumference: >35 in (88 cm) for women or 40 in (101 cm) for men.
  • Triglycerides levels: ≥150 mg/dL (1.7 mmol/L) or on triglyceride-lowering prescription.
  • HDL levels: <50 mg/dL (1.3 mmol/L) for women and <40 mg/dL (1.0 mmol/L) for men.
  • Blood pressure: ≥130 mmHg systolic BP and ≥85 mmHg diastolic BP or BP medication.
  • Fasting glucose: >100 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L) or on glucose-lowering prescription.

Metabolic Syndrome Criteria — Symptoms of Metabolic Syndrome

Although there are several models designed to diagnose metabolic syndrome, this metabolic syndrome calculator uses the model proposed by the American Heart Association and National Heart Lung and Blood Institute. This model focuses on five criteria, which involve these physiological and blood parameters:

  • Abdominal obesity: Waist circumference above 35 inches (88 cm) for women and 40 inches (101 cm) for men.
  • Triglycerides: Levels above 150 mg/dL (1.7 mmol/L) or a prescription for a triglyceride-lowering drug.
  • HDL cholesterol: Levels below 50 mg/dL (1.3 mmol/L) for women and 40 mg/dL (1.0 mmol/L) for men.
  • Blood pressure: Systolic pressure ≥130 mmHg and diastolic pressure ≥85 mmHg or medication for hypertension.
  • Fasting glucose levels: Glucose levels ≥100 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L) or a prescription for glucose-lowering medication.

You might wonder why exactly these criteria apply. Each one is linked to metabolic syndrome in a unique way, with its own set of symptoms and effects on the body.

When to see a doctor:

  • If you meet ≥3 criteria
  • If you have 1-2 criteria plus a family history of diabetes/heart disease
  • For medication options if lifestyle changes aren't enough

Treatment Recommendations

If you have metabolic syndrome or are at risk, consider these lifestyle changes:

  • Healthy diet: Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins
  • Regular exercise: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week
  • Weight management: Even modest weight loss can improve metabolic health
  • Stress management: Practice relaxation techniques
  • Quit smoking: Smoking worsens metabolic health
