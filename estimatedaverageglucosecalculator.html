Understanding the Estimated Average Glucose (eAG) Calculator

The eAG calculator is designed to help you translate your HbA1c (also called A1c) — a lab test that reflects average blood sugar — into a number that represents your estimated blood glucose over the past 3 months. This value is displayed in mg/dL or mmol/L, depending on your region or preference.

Key Parameters Explained

  • HbA1c (%): Represents the percentage of hemoglobin in your red blood cells that has glucose attached to it. This indicates how much sugar has been circulating in your blood over time.
  • Estimated Average Glucose (eAG): A conversion of your HbA1c value into an average daily blood glucose number, making it easier to understand for day-to-day monitoring.

How It Works

Glucose, a simple sugar, is your body’s main source of energy. When glucose levels rise, some of it sticks to hemoglobin in red blood cells. The more glucose present in your bloodstream, the higher the percentage of glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c). Because red blood cells live for around 3 to 4 months, this test gives a reliable average of your blood sugar over time.

Normal Ranges & What They Mean

  • HbA1c below 5.7%: Normal
  • HbA1c 5.7% – 6.4%: Prediabetes
  • HbA1c 6.5% and above: Diabetes

These ranges help doctors assess risk levels and determine if lifestyle changes or medications are needed.

Why eAG Matters

eAG gives people with diabetes a clearer view of their long-term blood sugar control in a format similar to daily blood glucose readings. It also helps track whether treatments and dietary efforts are working.
