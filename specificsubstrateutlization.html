Note:

This calculator determines the Specific Substrate Utilization Rate (q), which measures how efficiently microorganisms consume organic substrates in biological treatment systems.

It is widely used in wastewater treatment, biotechnology, and environmental engineering to assess microbial performance and optimize treatment processes.

Explanation of Parameters:

  • Sewage Inflow Rate (Q): The volume of sewage entering the treatment system per day (m³/day).
  • Influent Substrate Concentration (S₀): The organic matter concentration in the influent wastewater (mg/L).
  • Effluent Substrate Concentration (Sₑ): The organic matter concentration in the treated effluent (mg/L).
  • Volume of Aeration Tank (V): The total volume of the aeration tank where microbial treatment occurs (m³).
  • MLSS Concentration (X): The concentration of Mixed Liquor Suspended Solids (MLSS) in the aeration tank (mg/L).

Validations:

  • Applicability: This formula is applicable in biological treatment systems, activated sludge processes, and microbial growth studies.
  • Positive Values Only: All parameters must be positive to ensure valid biological and chemical interpretations.
  • Valid Range: The influent substrate concentration (S₀) must be greater than the effluent concentration (Sₑ).
  • Limitations: This calculation assumes steady-state conditions and does not account for variations in microbial community dynamics.
