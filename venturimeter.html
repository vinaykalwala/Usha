Venturi Meter Flow Calculator

Note:

Venturi Meter Equation calculates flow velocity based on pressure difference across a constriction. It utilizes Bernoulli's principle and continuity equation to provide accurate flow measurements with minimal energy loss.

Key Parameters:

  • Pₐ - Pᵦ (Pressure Difference): This is the pressure drop between the wide section (inlet) and the narrow section (throat) of the Venturi. It drives the fluid to accelerate through the throat.
  • ρ (Fluid Density in kg/m³): This is the mass per unit volume of the fluid. It influences how much the fluid accelerates when pressure changes.
  • Cᵥ (Velocity Coefficient): A correction factor (typically between 0.9 and 1.0) that accounts for energy losses like friction and turbulence. A value closer to 1.0 means a more efficient flow.
  • Aₐ / Aᵦ (Area Ratio): The ratio of the cross-sectional area at the inlet to the area at the throat. This ratio must be greater than 1 to cause a pressure drop and accelerate the flow.
  • gc (Unit Conversion Factor): A constant used to ensure unit consistency, typically 1 in SI units (kg·m/N·s²).
  • Vᵦ - Vₐ (Velocity Difference in m/s): The difference in fluid velocity between the throat (Vᵦ) and the inlet (Vₐ). This shows how much the fluid speeds up due to the pressure difference.

Important Considerations:

  • Requires laminar flow for maximum accuracy
  • Pressure taps must be properly located
  • Fluid must completely fill the pipe
  • Not suitable for slurries or dirty fluids
  • Calibration improves accuracy

Why This Formula is Important?

Venturi meters are among the most accurate flow measurement devices, essential for process control and environmental monitoring where precise flow data is required.

Industry Standards & Guidelines:

ISO 5167 for orifice plate measurements. ASME MFC-3M for liquid flow in closed conduits. EPA Method 2 for stack gas velocity measurements.

Conclusion:

The Venturi meter provides one of the most reliable methods for flow measurement in quality control and environmental applications. Its accuracy and minimal pressure loss make it ideal for applications where precise flow data is essential for process control or regulatory compliance.
