Understanding Your Dietary Reference Intake (DRI)

The **Dietary Reference Intake (DRI)** helps individuals estimate their daily **energy and nutrient needs** based on various personal factors such as **age, gender, weight, height, and activity level**. It is used to plan **healthy diets** that support **energy balance, muscle maintenance, and overall well-being**.

  • Energy Requirement: The estimated daily calorie intake needed to maintain body functions and activity levels.
  • Macronutrients: The recommended intake of **proteins, carbohydrates, and fats** to support body functions.
  • Metabolism Factor: Energy needs vary based on **individual metabolic rate and lifestyle**.

üî• How Energy Needs Are Estimated

The total energy required depends on two main factors:

  • Resting Energy Use: Energy required to sustain basic body functions, such as breathing and digestion.
  • Activity Energy Use: Additional calories required for movement, exercise, and daily tasks.

By combining these, we can estimate your total **daily energy expenditure (TDEE)**, which helps determine how much food energy you need daily.

Your daily energy intake is divided among three key macronutrients:

  • Proteins: Essential for **muscle repair, immune function, and hormone production**.
  • Carbohydrates: The primary source of **quick energy** for the body.
  • Fats: Support **cell function, hormone production, and long-term energy storage**.
