Recycling Carbon Footprint Savings Calculator

Select material and enter weight to calculate CO₂ savings:

How This Calculator Works

This calculator estimates the amount of CO₂ emissions you save by recycling different materials. The savings are based on CO₂ saving factors, which represent how much carbon footprint is reduced for each unit of material recycled.

  • Select a material from the dropdown list.
  • Enter the weight of the material you recycled.
  • Choose the unit of measurement (Kilograms or Pounds).
  • Click the "Calculate Savings" button to see the result.

How CO₂ Savings Are Estimated:

Every material contributes differently to CO₂ reduction when recycled. For example, recycling plastic prevents more emissions compared to glass, because plastic production releases higher amounts of CO₂.

The calculator checks the weight of the material you entered and multiplies it by a predefined CO₂ saving factor specific to that material. These factors are based on environmental studies and industry data.

Average CO₂ Savings by Material:

  • Plastic: ~2.26 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled
  • Paper: ~1.01 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled
  • Glass: ~0.68 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled
  • Metals: ~12.92 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled
  • Scrap Metal: ~7.87 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled
  • Aluminum: ~17.95 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled
  • Steel: ~1.89 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled
  • Copper: ~5.86 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled
  • Textiles: ~7.43 lbs CO₂ saved per pound recycled

If you recycle a large amount, the total CO₂ savings increase accordingly. This gives you an idea of how much you are reducing the environmental impact by recycling.
