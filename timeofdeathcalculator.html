Time of Death Calculator Overview

The Time of Death Calculator is a forensic tool used to estimate how much time has passed between a person’s death and the discovery of their body. This insight is crucial for investigations at both the crime scene and during autopsies.

Two Key Methods Used

  • Rigor Mortis: Based on the stiffening of muscles after death.
  • Algor Mortis: Based on the body's cooling rate post-death.

How to Use the Calculator

To get started, you’ll need two values:

  • Ambient Temperature: The temperature of the surroundings (can be entered in Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F), or Kelvin (K); it will be converted to Celsius internally).
  • Body Temperature: Measured rectally from the deceased body (can be entered in Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F), or Kelvin (K); it will be converted to Celsius internally).

Results are shown in hours. The calculator also supports reverse calculations for scenario analysis.

Estimating Time Using Algor Mortis

After death, the body cools to match room temperature. This cooling rate changes with environment: it’s faster in colder conditions (below 32°F or 0°C) and slower in warmer ones.

Estimating Time Using Rigor Mortis

Muscle stiffness begins a few hours after death, peaks, then fades. Use the chart below as a reference:

Body Condition Muscle Stiffness Estimated Time Since Death
Warm Flexible Less than 2 hours
Warm Rigid Between 2 and 8 hours
Cold Rigid Between 8 and 36 hours
Cold Flexible More than 36 hours

Note: If a hand is gripping something tightly (e.g., weapon), it may suggest the item was placed postmortem.

When Is Time of Death Determined?

Time of death is assessed in unexplained deaths or where timing is vital. Investigators use both field observations and autopsy analysis.

Other Methods to Estimate Time of Death

  • Neuromuscular response to physical or chemical stimuli.
  • Tissue drying – like lips and eyes.
  • Livor Mortis – Skin discoloration due to blood pooling.
  • Odor and bloating during decomposition.

FAQs

What is rigor mortis?
Muscle stiffening post-death due to ATP loss.

When does it start?
Begins after ~2 hours, peaks around 6–8 hours, fades by 24–36 hours.

What is algor mortis?
The cooling of the body until it matches the environment.

What Happens After Death?

  1. Pallor Mortis – Pale skin.
  2. Algor Mortis – Cooling.
  3. Rigor Mortis – Muscle stiffness.
  4. Livor Mortis – Blood pooling.
  5. Putrefaction – Onset of decomposition.
  6. Decomposition – Breakdown of soft tissues.
  7. Skeletonization – Final stage.

CPR Window

CPR may be successful if started within 4–6 minutes after death. In hypothermia cases, it can be extended until the body warms to 32°C (89.6°F).
