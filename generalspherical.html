Settling Velocity Calculator(General-Spherical)

Note:

This calculator determines the Terminal Settling Velocity, which is crucial in fluid mechanics for predicting the settling behavior of spherical particles in a fluid.

It is widely used in sedimentation analysis, wastewater treatment, and industrial separation processes to ensure efficiency and accuracy.

Settling-VelocitySettling velocity is the speed at which a particle falls through a fluid due to gravity, influenced by particle size, density, fluid viscosity, and flow conditions

Explanation of Parameters:

  • Density of Particle (ρₚ): The mass per unit volume of the settling particle, measured in kg/m³.
  • Density of Fluid (ρ_f): The mass per unit volume of the fluid medium, measured in kg/m³.
  • Diameter of Sphere (d): The size of the particle, measured in meters.
  • Viscosity of Fluid (μ): The fluid's resistance to deformation, measured in Pa·s.
  • Terminal Settling Velocity (vₜ): The constant velocity at which a particle settles in a fluid due to gravity.
  • Reynolds Number (Re): A dimensionless value that determines the flow regime around the particle.
  • Drag Coefficient (C_D): A factor that accounts for resistance experienced by the particle while settling.

Why Terminal Settling Velocity is Important?

Terminal settling velocity helps in **designing sedimentation tanks, optimizing industrial separation processes, and analyzing environmental sediment transport**.

Validations:

  • Applicability: This formula applies to spherical particles settling in a fluid.
  • Valid Density Values: The particle density must be greater than the fluid density for settling to occur.
  • Valid Diameter and Viscosity: These values must be positive and realistic to ensure accurate calculations.
  • Limitations: This calculation assumes a single, non-interacting spherical particle in a uniform fluid.

Real-life Applications:

  • Wastewater Treatment: Optimizing sedimentation tanks for particle separation.
  • Mining & Mineral Processing: Determining the settling rates of particles in slurry systems.
  • Environmental Studies: Analyzing sediment transport in rivers and lakes.
  • Pharmaceutical Industry: Understanding the suspension behavior of drug formulations.
  • Food Industry: Predicting the separation of solid particles in liquid food processing.
