Quantity of heat transferred by condensing steam

Note:

This calculator is used in environmental or energy-efficiency assessments to determine the heat released when steam condenses. You simply provide:

Input Guidelines:

  • Mass of Steam: Enter the actual mass of steam in kilograms (e.g., 100 kg).
  • Specific Enthalpy of Evaporation: Use the value provided in technical datasheets (commonly around 2257 kJ/kg for water at 100°C), or any other value specific to your system.

The calculator multiplies these values to give you the total heat (in kJ) released upon condensation. This is crucial for processes such as industrial heating, boiler systems, or steam-based energy recovery.

Real-Life Application:

In industrial settings like power plants or manufacturing facilities, boilers generate steam for various processes. The heat recovered from condensing steam can be reused to preheat feedwater or other process fluids, reducing energy consumption and operational costs, and enhancing overall system efficiency.
