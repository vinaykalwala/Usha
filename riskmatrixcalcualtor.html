Risk Matrix Calculator

The Risk Matrix (Numeric Representation) categorizes risks based on probability and consequence, helping assess workplace hazards effectively.

Usage and Application

  • Workplace Safety Assessments: Evaluating hazards in industrial, construction, and manufacturing environments
  • Project Risk Management: Assessing potential risks in project planning phases
  • Process Hazard Analysis: Identifying and prioritizing risks in chemical and operational processes
  • Job Safety Analysis (JSA): Quantifying risks associated with specific job tasks
  • Regulatory Compliance: Meeting OSHA, ISO 31000, and other safety standard requirements

Risk Matrix Interpretation

Risk Score (R = P × C) Risk Level Action Required
1-4 Low Risk Acceptable, monitor as needed
5-10 Medium Risk Mitigation required
11-25 High Risk Immediate control measures needed

Probability Scale Guide

  • 1 - Very Unlikely: May occur only in exceptional circumstances (<10% chance)
  • 2 - Unlikely: Could occur sometime (10-30% chance)
  • 3 - Possible: Might occur occasionally (30-50% chance)
  • 4 - Likely: Will probably occur (50-70% chance)
  • 5 - Very Likely: Expected to occur frequently (>70% chance)

Consequence Scale Guide

  • 1 - Negligible: No injuries, minimal financial loss
  • 2 - Minor: First aid injury, minor equipment damage
  • 3 - Moderate: Medical treatment, moderate downtime
  • 4 - Major: Serious injury, major equipment loss
  • 5 - Catastrophic: Fatality, extensive damage
