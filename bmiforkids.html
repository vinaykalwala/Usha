BMI Calculator for Kids

Note:

This BMI Calculator for Kids helps assess whether a child's weight is appropriate for their height and age. It provides BMI, BMI prime, and percentile ranking compared to other children of the same age and gender.

Key Inputs:

  • Weight: In kilograms (1 kg = 2.2 lbs)
  • Height: In meters (1 m = 3.28 ft)
  • Age: Between 2 and 20 years
  • Sex: Biological sex for accurate percentile calculation

How It Works:

The calculator uses the standard BMI formula adjusted for children's growth patterns. Results show:

  • BMI: Weight/Height² (kg/m²)
  • BMI Prime: BMI divided by 25 (adult ideal)
  • Percentile: Comparison with same-age children
  • Category: Weight status classification

BMI Categories for Children:

BMI Category Percentile Range
Underweight Below 5th percentile
Healthy 5th to 85th percentile
Overweight 85th to 95th percentile
Obese Above 95th percentile

Important Considerations:

  • Growth spurts can temporarily affect BMI measurements
  • Muscle mass and body composition vary significantly
  • BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic measure
  • Always consult a pediatrician for health assessments

What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measurement of the body's mass that takes into account not just the weight of a person, but also their height. The acronym BMI stands for Body Mass Index, and it is very easy to calculate on your own, though it's even easier if you use our BMI calculator for kids.

BMI for Kids: Why It Matters

BMI (Body Mass Index) is used to assess if a child’s weight is within a healthy range based on their age and sex. Since children grow at different rates, BMI is compared using percentiles.

BMI Charts for Boys

Due to differences in body composition, growth spurts, and development phases, BMI values fluctuate widely among children. Factors like muscle mass, fat distribution, and hydration levels can influence BMI, making percentiles essential for accurate assessment. To determine if a boy's BMI is within a healthy range, doctors compare his BMI to standardized BMI charts that aggregate data from children of the same age.

BMI Charts for Girls

Similarly, BMI charts for girls are used to evaluate weight status in relation to peers. These charts allow doctors to determine whether a child falls within a healthy range or if further evaluation is needed. A child is generally considered to have a healthy BMI if they fall between the 5th and 95th percentile of their age group.
