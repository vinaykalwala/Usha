Rapid mix and flocculator design

Rapid mix and flocculator design are essential for water treatment. The rapid mix stage (10-30 sec, G=300-1000 s⁻¹) ensures coagulants are evenly dispersed to destabilize particles. The flocculation stage (15-45 min, G=20-80 s⁻¹) gently grows these particles into settleable flocs. The Gt value (10⁴-10⁵) guides design to balance mixing intensity and duration. Engineers use parameters like power input (P), viscosity (μ), and tank volume (V) to optimize equipment and meet water quality standards, ensuring efficient, cost-effective treatment

Explanation of Parameters:

  • Power (P): Energy input to the fluid, measured in N·m/s or ft-lb/sec.
  • Dynamic Viscosity (μ): Fluid's resistance to flow, measured in Pa·s or lb/(ft-sec).
  • Volume (V): Total fluid volume being mixed, measured in m³ or ft³.
  • Head Loss (HL): Energy loss due to mixing, measured in m or ft.
  • Time (t): Duration of mixing, measured in seconds.
  • Velocity Gradient (G): Mixing intensity, calculated in s⁻¹.
  • Gt Value: Dimensionless design parameter for flocculation.

Why Velocity Gradient is Important?

Velocity gradient helps in optimizing treatment processes, ensuring effective chemical mixing, and preventing inefficient floc formation or particle breakup.
