About Injury Severity Score (ISS)

The Injury Severity Score (ISS) is a medical score used to assess trauma severity in patients who have sustained injuries in multiple body regions. It helps determine how critical a patient's condition is and is used in trauma research, emergency care, and decision-making.

Using the Calculator

Select the worst injury level for each of the six body regions listed. These include:

  • Head and Neck (including cervical spine)
  • Face (facial skeleton, mouth, eyes, ears, nose)
  • Chest (including thoracic spine and diaphragm)
  • Abdomen (abdominal organs, lumbar spine, pelvic contents)
  • Extremities (including pelvic girdle)
  • External (skin and surface injuries)

Each region is rated on a scale from 0 (no injury) to 6 (unsurvivable injury). After selecting scores for all regions, the ISS is calculated by squaring the three highest scores and summing them

Important: If any region is scored a 6, the ISS is automatically set to 75 (the maximum possible score), indicating a critical and unsurvivable condition.

Interpretation of ISS

  • 0–8: Minor Injury
  • 9–15: Moderate Injury
  • 16–24: Severe Injury
  • 25 or more: Very Severe / Profound Trauma

The ISS helps predict mortality, hospitalization time, and treatment urgency. An ISS above 15 is generally considered to indicate major trauma requiring immediate medical attention.

Clinical Significance

The ISS is the only anatomical scoring system that correlates linearly with patient outcomes such as:

  • Mortality
  • Morbidity
  • Length of hospital stay
  • Need for specialized trauma care

This calculator is intended for educational and preliminary screening purposes. Final assessment and treatment should always be done by trained healthcare professionals.
