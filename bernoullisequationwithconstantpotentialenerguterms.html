Bernoulli's Equation With Constant Potential Energy Terms Calculator

Note:

Bernoulli's Equation: This equation explains the relationship between pressure and velocity in a steady fluid flow system. It states that the total energy (pressure + kinetic energy) remains constant.

Parameters:

  • P₁ and P₂: Initial and final pressure in Pascals (Pa).
  • U₁ and U₂: Initial and final velocity in meters per second (m/s).
  • ρ (Density): Fluid density in kg/m³.

Real-life Applications:

  • Fire Safety: Used to determine pressure and flow in firefighting water pipelines.
  • Aerospace: Explains lift generation in airplane wings.
  • Medical: Used in devices like blood pressure monitors.

Conclusion:

Bernoulli’s Equation is essential in fluid dynamics, ensuring safety in firefighting, aviation, and medical applications.
