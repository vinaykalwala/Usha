Flow Rate Calculator (Crane Equation 3-20)

Note:

Crane Equation 3-20 calculates compressible fluid flow through valves, fittings and pipes. The equation accounts for expansion effects and determines if flow is sonic (choked) or subsonic.

Key Parameters That Affect Flow Rate:

  • Expansion Factor (Y): Adjusts for compressibility effects in high-pressure flows.
  • Diameter (d): Affects the cross-sectional area available for flow.
  • Pressure Drop (ΔP): Higher pressure difference increases the flow rate.
  • Loss Coefficient (K): Accounts for resistance from fittings, valves, and pipe roughness.
  • Temperature (T₁): Affects gas density and flow characteristics.
  • Specific Gravity (Sg): Ensures corrections for gas properties relative to air.

Real-Life Applications:

  • Oil & Gas Pipelines: Ensures safe transportation of natural gas.
  • Power Plants: Determines steam flow through turbines and boilers.
  • Fire Safety Systems: Calculates gas flow rates in suppression systems.

Why Venting is Important?

Proper venting ensures safe pressure relief in piping systems, preventing equipment failure and hazardous conditions.
