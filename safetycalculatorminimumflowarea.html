AD-Merkblatt valves - Minimum flow area for Dry Gases And Air

Note:

This formula calculates the minimum flow area (A₀) for dry gases and air in safety valves. It ensures that gases are vented safely without causing dangerous pressure buildup. Used in industrial safety systems to prevent system failures due to excessive pressure.

Parameter Breakdown:

  • A₀ (mm²): Minimum flow area required for safe discharge.
  • ṁ: Mass flow rate (kg/h).
  • Ψ: Outflow function (dimensionless).
  • αw: Outflow coefficient (efficiency factor).
  • PR: Absolute relieving pressure (bar a).
  • T: Inlet temperature (Kelvin).
  • M: Molar mass (kg/kmol).
  • Z: Compressibility factor (accounts for real gas behavior).
  • 0.1791: A constant derived from thermodynamic principles, used to normalize units and account for standard gas flow conditions in the equation.

AD-Merkblatt (Arbeitsgemeinschaft Druckbehälter) is a German standard that provides guidelines for the design, construction, and testing of pressure vessels to ensure safety and compliance with European regulations.

Real-Life Applications:

  • Industrial Safety Valves: Prevents overpressure in boilers and pipelines.
  • Fire Protection Systems: Ensures safe steam release in emergencies.
  • Environmental Compliance: Helps industries regulate steam and gas emissions.

Conclusion:

This formula helps in designing safe and efficient pressure relief systems. By calculating the correct flow area, it protects equipment and workers.
